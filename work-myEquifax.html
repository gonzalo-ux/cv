myEquifax.com

myEquifax.com is a platform that delivers free access to credit score twice a year and 6 credit reports a year. It allows customers to upsell to Core Credit which means a free monthly subscription for credit score and credit report. And from there, customers can upgrade to paid products such as Equifax Credit Monitor and Equifax Complete just launched this summer.

I worked as part of a group of internal and external ux designers.

My role

After the first releases of the GCS design system, the team in the US asked me to collaborate more directly with the product team. From that point, I’d be accountable for:

  • The design system maintenance and progress.
  • New design work for myEquifax Premium product and to translate US design to the Canada and Australia context.
  • Review and guide design decisions from third-party vendors to comply with best practices and the design system.

Signin page

Although I wasn’t not in charge of the signing funnel I reviewed analytics data and I found we had almost 35% of signing fails. There was no initiative to attack this problem so I started to work on how to improve the signing experience and to reduce the failings using quick research methods and evaluating possible solutions that would demand low effort and big impact.

I mapped the whole flow, from setting the password to the first signing. The application has some demanding rules for password creation due to security concerns that are out of my reach so I needed to work inside this frame.

My first assumption was that it is too difficult to remember this kind of password due to these requirements. But what was the difference with another kind of password that users will remember effectively?

MyEquifax was being used in the US and I was far away here in Dublin, but the experience would be pretty similar, which I’d found out later. Using a mock, I set a short scenario where a user would need to create the account, following the user and password requirements and then trying to signin manually for the first time. I discovered that most of the time they were failing at the creation of the password and then at the login, they were using special characters not allowed in the password ruleset.

I worked with different alternatives and finally I prototyped a password reveal component that might help users to have lees errors and this way improve the success signing. I tested icon and text buttons and checkboxes and switches to activate the show/hide event. I considered translation to different languages that could impact the length of the text button. I don’t have access for A/B testing which would have been my choice to go. But after testing with random users we landed on the simplest and more direct idea.

After building and launching the best version base on our tests we reduce the signing errors from 36% to 10% which means we reduce call centre and customer support cost in a considerable amount.

After launching we gather more feedback from users that help us to quickly fix bugs especially after observing users navigation the signing with Screen Readers which led us to improve tab index navigation and how the message shall be read out when using assistive technology.

If I had access to A/B testing we would have been able to experiment with other alternatives and maybe decrease the signing failures even more.

Accessibility improvements

Through my work in the Design System for GCS I helped to improve accessibility aspects especially colour contrast issues. I provided a set of guides and tokens so developers and designers could build with clear colour contrast in sync, especially in case of errors and or important information.

I designed and implemented a consistent focus treatment for all our UI components, therefore improving the navigation experience for users that navigate using exclusively the keyboard.
