myEquifax.com

myEquifax.comis a platform that delivers free access to credit score twice a year and 6 credit reports a year. It allows customers to upsell to Core Credit which means a free monthly subscription for credit score and credit report. And from there, customers can upgrade to paid products such as Equifax Credit Monitor and Equifax Complete just launched this summer.

Signin page

Although I wasn’t not in charge of the signing funnel I reviewed analytics data and I found we had almost 35% of signing fails. There was no initiative to attack this problem so I started to work on how to improve the signing experience and to reduce the failings using quick research methods and evaluating possible solutions that would demand low effort and big impact.

I mapped the whole flow, from setting the password to the first signing. The application has some demanding rules for password creation due to security concerns that are out of my reach so I needed to work inside this frame. My first assumption was that it is too difficult to remember this kind of password due to these requirements. But what was the difference with another kind of password that users will remember effectively?

MyEquifax was being used in the US and I was far away here in Dublin, but the experience would be pretty similar, which I’d found out later. Using a mock, I set a short scenario where a user would need to create the account, following the user and password requirements and then trying to login manually for the first time. I discovered that most of the time they were failing at the creation of the password and then at the login, they were using special characters not allowed in the password ruleset.

So I prototype a password reveal component that we tested and help users to have lees errors, I tested icon vs text variations. I don’t have access for A/B testing which would have been my choice to go. But after building and launching the best tested version we reduce the signing errors from 36% to 10% which means we reduce call center cost by about $xxxx. Happy story at the end.
