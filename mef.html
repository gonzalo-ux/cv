MEF

In 2020 my partner and I started to work with the Universidad de Santiago, Chile to improve their webpage for the M.S. in Finances and Economics .

Problem:
The website looked old and with outdated information which confuses applicants to the master redirecting all the queries to emails and calls.
“Master in Economy” search term showed the website number 15 in the second page.

Team:
Eliette Hermosilla, Researcher & Content Designer
Gonzalo Vasquez, Product designer & Front-end developer

My role

This project was made 100% online.
I lead the product design work and front-end implementation, my partner Eliette H. carried the content design work, we divided research tasks. I set a Google Analytics account for the website, and I installed a script to track metrics in Hotjar. I designed the visual elements of the website and had to implement styles and pages working with Joomla to build the pages and manage the content. I also worked setting interviews and tests with participants online back in Chile.

Research

We started by auditing the content from our audience in order to define a common language.

Using Google Trends we searched, compared and registered the most used terms from our audience. We built the first Glossary terms that added to interviews helped us to define Voice + Tone, labeling strategy for micro-copy and design short and meaningful headings to navigate the content. This set of elements was documented in a Content guidelines(Spanish), made by Eliette Hermosilla, to be used in the future by the Master’s office.

Google analytics and Hotjar allowed us to map our first data about user navigation and behaviour in the website. These help us to concentrate the efforts prioritizing the pages with heavy navigation and the key elements that would allow someone to send an application. The number of applications would be our main business goal to track during the project.

On another hand, we did a full review of queries to the support email of the Master, which gave us a sense of the problems that users might face when using the current website and which was the information that was missing or confusing them to achieve their applications. It also helped us to contrast with the list of terms that we had form Google trend searches, we ended up changin many terms based on what we found in the emails.

After reviewing, organizing and filtering almost 300 queries in a spreadsheet we ended up building three profiles:

  • Undergraduates of the same University
  • Undergraduate of a different University
  • Undergraduate from a different country in South America

We combined this with analytics of users flows to focus the energy to make clear that information related to Courses details of the Master, entry requirements and application process.

One of our findings was that users were always looking for key pieces of information to decide to apply to the Master such as:

  • Important dates such as application timeframe and the start of classes
  • Cost of the Master and scholarships
  • Schedules
  • What to do in case of doubts

Design and implementation

We had a short time frame, so I moved quickly to design and validate with figma prototypes as we interviewed former and current students from the M.S. and also gathered feedback from engineers that were looking for similar M.S. We assembled these users in groups and we tested our assumptions with some of them representing different flows and states.

Based on our research we find that a user could be:

  1. In an early stage looking for a Master
  2. With an inclination for this kind of Master, but unsure about which program to apply to
  3. Already decided for this program, but uncertain about how to apply and what are the requirements.

For these three scenarios, It was necessary to structure the information and content, grouping chunks of information with enough value to understand the process and links to navigate to a specific section. This helped to reduce the confusion and guide the applicants for the right path.

We setted a permanent aside section with all the information that was most requested along with a CTA to start the application and contact information with the Master offices.

I defined a new color scheme based on an approved and used brochure document from the M.S and we build design elements for social networks.

The current page was running in a Joomla CMS which was far from my knowledge. But I got to manage and did the css and html work. The main issue was to install joomla and used to build locally but it never worked. Instead I used local overrides with Google chrome so I could push directly to the browser with my local set of Sass and CSS files.

Outcomes

The Master duplicated the amount of new students even with the COVID pandemic in between, thanks to the website improvements and a small email marketing campaign. The website went up to the 5fth place in Google searches and increased significantly the pages visited and the amount of time of users. We reduced the emails and calls with queries regarding to basic information of the Master.
