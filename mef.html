MEF

In 2020 my partner and I started to work with the Universidad de Santiago, Chile to improve their webpage for the M.S. in Finances and Economics .

Problem:
The website looked old and with outdated information which confuses applicants to the master redirecting all the queries to emails and calls.
“Master in Economy” search term showed the website number 15 in the second page.

Team:
Eliette Hermosilla, Researcher & Content Designer
Gonzalo Vasquez, Product designer & Front-end developer

My role

I lead the product design work and front-end implementation, my partner carried the content design work, we divided research tasks. I set a Google Analytics account for the website, and I installed a script to track metrics in Hotjar. I designed the visual elements of the website and had to implement styles and pages working with Joomla to build the pages and manage the content. I also worked setting interviews and tests with participants online back in Chile.

Research

We started by auditing the content from our audience in order to define a common language.

Using Google Trends, G analytics and Hotjar we were able to map our first personas, we had their navigation flow and behaviour in the main pages. These help us to concentrate the efforts prioritizing the pages with heavy navigation and the key elements that would allow someone to send an application. The number of applications would be our main business goal to track during the project.

On another hand, we did a full review of queries to the support email of the Master, which gave us a sense of the problems that users might face when using the current website and which was the information that was missing or confusing them to achieve their applications.

With ended up building three profiles:

  • Undergraduates of the same University
  • Undergraduate of a different University
  • Undergraduate from a different country in South America

Design and implementation

We had a short time frame, so I moved quickly to design and validate with figma prototypes as we interviewed former and current students from the M.S. and also gathered feedback from engineers that were looking for similar M.S. We assembled these users in groups and we tested our assumptions with some of them representing different flows and states.

Based on our research we find that a user could be:

  1. In an early stage looking for a Master
  2. With an inclination for this kind of Master, but unsure about which program to apply to
  3. Already decided for this program, but uncertain about how to apply and what are the requirements.

This was necessary to structure the information and content, with chunks of information with enough value to understand the process and links to navigate to a specific section.

I defined a new color scheme based on an approved and used brochure document from the M.S and we build design elements for social networks.

The current page was running in a Joomla CMS which was far from my knowledge. But I got to manage and did the css and html work. The main issue was to install joomla and used to build locally but it never worked. Instead I used local overrides with Google chrome so I could push directly to the browser with my local set of Sass and CSS files.

Outcomes

The Master duplicated the amount of new students even with the COVID pandemic in between. The website went up to the 5fth place in Google and increased significantly the pages visited and the amount of time of users.
